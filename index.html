Christina Rossetti is one of the best female poets of all time. Born in England in 1830, and started composing verses before she
could write and wrote her first full poem when she was 11. Most of her poetic works reflect her Anglo-Catholic faith.
She is perhaps best known for her ‘Goblin Market’ but she has also written more than 300 other works.
'Vanity of Vanities' is an example of her faith based poetic works.
It is based on the book of Ecclesiastes.

Vanity of vanities, the Preacher saith,
All things are vanity. The eye and ear
Cannot be filled with what they see and hear.
Like early dew, or like the sudden breath
Of wind, or like the grass that withereth,
Is man, tossed to and fro by hope and fear:
So little joy hath he, so little cheer,
Till all things end in the long dust of death.
To-day is still the same as yesterday,
To-morrow also even as one of them;
And there is nothing new under the sun:
Until the ancient race of Time be run,
The old thorns shall grow out of the old stem,
And morning shall be cold and twilight grey.

She continued to be a prolific poet until her death in 1894.
